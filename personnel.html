Who We Are

Neil


I am an Interactive Digital Media student with a background in music, audio production and English literature. My role in the Into Kilmainham project has been audio designer and audio programmer. Our game uses a cutting-edge spatial audio API called 3Dception, which places and processes sounds in virtual space relative to the player’s head movements. I recorded a number of actors and produced appropriate sound effects and ambiences for each scene in the game. I then designed and wrote the C# scripts that load and trigger the audio Assets sequentially as the player moves through the game world. My hope is that the 3D audio enhances the immersion of the virtual Kilmainham experience.




Conor


My main role in the Into Kilmainham Research Project was 3D modelling. I modelled virtually all of the architecture and many of the in-game objects in Maya (with help from James, who was also responsible for all of the texturing). Once the modelling was complete my main responsibility was producing an animation to show off the various areas in the game, using higher quality lighting, dynamics and rendering than what is possible to run in real-time in the actual game. I was also involved in finalising the game’s narrative and writing the script with Philip (with input from the rest of the team). Overall I found the work very enjoyable and educational, and surprisingly not as horrifically stressful as some of our previous projects.




James


I’m James, and I have a background in business and the arts. I previously studied English and worked in various roles in hotels for some time before pursuing a career in computing and interactive media, through the M.Sc. Interactive Digital Media course. I have always had a love for games or any form of visually engrossing media and focused my thesis on Google Cardboard and Google Glass. Luckily I was paired with a group of the finest individuals for this project who shared that passion and the end result is an immersive game which we loved creating. Hopefully you’ll find it reflects this and enjoy the experience.

Alina


My name is Alina and I personally have an international background, both Russian and German; and career wise – film, journalistic and digital marketing experience. After living in Berlin, Kiev and Moscow I have chosen Dublin as my new home and M.Sc. Interactive Digital Media at Trinity College – as a new direction for my professional path. I am a passionate gamer and was delighted to become a project manager of our final study project, which happened to be a VR Google Cardboard game. I was lucky to work in a great team and what we have achieved is an ambitious but nonetheless a successful result. Thank you guys!

Phil


I came to Interactive Digital Media because I really like making things, but lack the upper body strength for manual labour. I have backgrounds in film production and multimedia creation, so getting the chance to work on a game has been an incredible continuation into the forefront of digital entertainment. Programming and designing an immersive experience with a talented bunch of lads has been a rewarding and enriching experience. Exploring virtual reality has been particularly exciting for me, and is something I look forward to experimenting more with in the future.
